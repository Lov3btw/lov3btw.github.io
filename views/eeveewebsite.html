Project Overview

Eevee Umbreon is a jewelry designer who wants to make her products available to online consumers. All users should expect an enjoyable shopping and purchasing experience. Client wants all users to feel secure and confident throughout their whole experience on the website.

Project duration:

November 2022 - January 2023

The problem:

Our client requested a website to ensure a pleasant shopping experience that would set them apart from their competitors.

The goal:

Offering a pleasant shopping experience that is accessible to all users and offer a secure process to everyone who might be skeptical of purchasing luxury goods online.

My role:

Lead UX designer, UX researcher

Responsibilities:

User research, Wire-framing, Prototyping

An unmoderated usability study was conducted remotely on December 13th. We took a qualitative approach in our primary interviews. The results of those interviews showed that accessibility and security were important issues among users, as well as a simple checkout process. We conducted a usability study followed up with a Systems Usability Scale.

User Pain Points

No way to save items for later

All users mentioned they often save items for later or delete them from the cart or “liking” items for comparison and/or future consideration.

Return to Homepage

While most users knew to click the websites logo to return home some did not.

Card spacing on the Homepage

All users in the study expressed confusion with the menu. We determined that moving accessibiltiy to the menu and simplifying the menu content would be benficial.

Icons

It was noted by some users that the icons came across as harsh and unbalanced.

Research

The target audience were individuals who enjoy and prefer shopping online to save time and access more options.

Round 1 findings

Added an icon for backwards navigation and also adjusted all other icons to better fit the tone of the website.

Adjust spacing specifically in the category cards and their corresponding text

Add a “Save for later” and “Delete” button in the cart for each item. Also adding the option for users to “like” an item while shopping.

I made two size variations as you can see. My client wanted users to easily shop anywhere they were, on whatever device they might be using, with however much time they may have to browse. The only difference in the size variations is we removed some of the icons from the navigation bar. All those features and settings can be found under the menu.

High Fidelity Prototype

For the High fidelity prototype we added the "saved" items screen and implemented the change to the menu and its features as well as removing icons from the homepage. I added the "Buy Now" option and added a quantity drop box.

Prototyping the hi-fi mock-up revealed a crucial design flaw that we were able to quickly rectify, which was the absence of an actual bag screen instead of utilizing just the bag module.

Takeaways

Impact:

What I Learned:

Going into this project I often found that I was overthinking everything from content to design. With advice from peers and independent research I was able to integrate design elements and features I had initially overlooked. Thus improving the visual design and functionality of the app.

Moving Forward

Conduct a follow-up usability study on the accessibility features and iterate on those findings.

Iterate on the best option for keeping users as up to date as possible on their order status and shipping details.
